Unlimited Zoom Meetings for Students

By Akshit Anchan | On 24 Sep 2020



Hi. This post will explain how teachers and students can get free unlimited Zoom accounts (without the 40-minute time limit). For this you will require an email ID with access to the mail application. Want to get right into it? Click here.

Curious how? Let me give you some background. Owing to the global COVID-19 pandemic, in the month of March, Zoom began lifting the 40-minute meeting limit on free Basic accounts for several countries. The rules to this are simple.

A school administrator submits an request via the Zoom K-12 School Verification Form. Zoom takes a look at the application and verifies the integrity of the platform - if it is an educational institute or not. If and when verified, the administrator gets a notification on their Zoom profile and that's it! Only one request is necessary per domain. For example, if the admin's email is admin@k12school.org, once itâ€™s approved the limit will be lifted for anyone who has a free Zoom account using a k12school.org email address.

Anyone else who registers using an email address associated with that school gets the same privileges. What privileges you ask?

  • Unlimited meetings for up to 100 participants
  • HD audio and video
  • Screen sharing
  • Whiteboarding
  • Annotation
  • Breakout Rooms
  • Virtual backgrounds
  • In-meeting chat
  • Local recording
  • Nonverbal feedback

So how do you get this feature?

  1. Make sure you have access to your school email.
  2. Go to zoom.us/signup and enter your date of birth for age verification.
  3. You'll be directed to a page to enter your email address under the School's Domain (for example name@k12school.org). DO NOT enter an @gmail, @yahoo, @outlook or such generic email addresses. Only use the one provided by the school. Requests from personal email addresses are not eligible. Once you enter it and finish the CAPTCHA test, you should recieve a verification email (to the school email address you provided).
    Note: Generally, you must go to outlook.live.com (applicable for IHS students). Log in with the credentials given by the school.
  4. Log in to the email you have provided and click on the verification link.
  5. Next, Zoom MAY ask you if you are signing up on behalf of a school. If you are a student click No, and if you are a teacher click Yes.
  6. Finally, proceed to fill in your information as required by Zoom - Name, Email, Password etc.

And that's it! Go to zoom.us/profile and you should see a green banner confirming the removal of the 40-minute limit.
